Fried Chicken

Fried chicken, also called Southern fried chicken, is a dish consisting of chicken pieces that have been coated with seasoned flour or batter and pan-fried, deep fried, pressure fried, or air fried. The breading adds a crisp coating or crust to the exterior of the chicken while retaining juices in the meat. Broiler chickens are most commonly used.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Directions

  1. Preheat oil in a deep-fryer or large cast-iron skillet to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Combine flour, garlic powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper in a large bowl.
  3. Place buttermilk in a separate bowl.
  4. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk, then dredge in flour mixture.
  5. Fry chicken in the preheated oil until golden brown and no longer pink in the center, about 20 minutes per side.
  6. Transfer chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.

Enjoy your delicious fried chicken!
